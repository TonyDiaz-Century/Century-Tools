Roofing

Roof replacement, repairs, materials, warranties, and roof-to-solar planning.

How much does a new roof cost in the Bay Area?

Roof cost depends on size, material, access, slope, tear-off conditions, and waterproofing complexity. The real difference is not just price—it is whether the roof is being installed to last.

How do I know if I need a repair or full replacement?

If the roof is older, has multiple leak points, failing underlayment, or widespread deterioration, replacement is usually the smarter long-term option. Repairs make sense when the system is still fundamentally sound.

How long does a roof last in California?

Composition roofs commonly last around 20–30 years, tile roofs can last 30–50+ years depending heavily on the underlayment, and properly installed PVC flat roofs can perform for 30+ years.

What are signs my roof is failing?

Common signs include active leaks, cracked or slipping roofing materials, soft decking, failed flashings, dry rot, and repeated patchwork repairs.

How long does a roof replacement take?

Most residential roof replacements take about 5–10 working days, depending on size, access, complexity, weather, and whether hidden issues are uncovered during tear-off.

What is the best roofing material for Bay Area homes?

There is no single best material for every home. Tile, composition, metal, and PVC each have their place. The best system depends on the structure, design, budget, and how long you plan to stay in the home.

What are the advantages of PVC roofing?

PVC is one of the strongest flat roof systems available. The seams are heat welded, it handles ponding water well, and it offers excellent long-term waterproofing performance when installed correctly.

Are tile roofs better than shingles?

Tile roofs can offer longer service life and a premium appearance, but they also require proper structure, careful details, and quality underlayment. The best choice depends on the house and the goals of the project.

What separates a high-quality roof from a cheap one?

The most important difference is in the details—flashings, penetrations, transitions, waterproofing methods, ventilation, and workmanship. Those details determine whether a roof lasts or becomes a recurring problem.

What is the biggest mistake homeowners make when choosing a roofer?

Choosing primarily by price. Low bids often leave out critical waterproofing details, labor time, and proper scope that are necessary for long-term performance.

Solar

Solar production, system upgrades, batteries, and utility strategy.

Is solar still worth it in California?

Yes, but the economics have changed. Today, the best systems are designed around self-consumption, smart load management, and battery strategy rather than relying only on exporting extra power.

What is the difference between NEM 2 and NEM 3?

NEM 2 generally provides stronger export value, while NEM 3 places more emphasis on using your own power efficiently and pairing solar with batteries.

Can I keep my NEM 2 plan if I upgrade my system?

Sometimes, but it depends on the type and size of the changes. Any proposed modification should be evaluated carefully before work begins.

How much power will my system produce?

Production depends on roof orientation, tilt, shading, panel count, panel type, inverter design, and local conditions. Every system should be evaluated specifically for that property.

Does shade really matter?

Yes. Even partial shading can affect production, which is why proper layout and equipment selection matter.

Do I need a battery with solar?

Not always, but batteries are becoming more valuable for backup power, better energy control, and improved use of your solar production.

Electrical

Panels, service upgrades, safety concerns, and troubleshooting.

Do I need to upgrade my panel for solar or EV charging?

Many homes do, but not all. It depends on the existing service size, panel condition, available capacity, and total load profile of the property.

What is the difference between a 200A and 400A service?

A 400A service offers substantially more electrical capacity and future flexibility, especially for larger homes or properties with solar, batteries, EV charging, or added loads.

How do I know if my electrical panel is outdated or unsafe?

Warning signs can include overheating, corrosion, known problematic equipment, overcrowding, physical damage, or insufficient capacity for modern needs.

Why does my breaker keep tripping?

A breaker may trip because of overload, a short circuit, a ground fault, or a problem with the connected equipment. It should not be ignored.

EV Charging

Home charging, panel capacity, charger sizing, and future-ready energy planning.

How much does it cost to install an EV charger?

Cost depends on charger size, distance from the panel, wall access, trenching if needed, permit requirements, and whether a panel upgrade is necessary.

Do I need a panel upgrade for EV charging?

Not always. Some homes have enough available capacity, while others need load management or an electrical service upgrade.

What size charger do I need?

That depends on your vehicle, daily driving, and how fast you want to recharge. Many homeowners choose 40A to 60A charging for a strong balance of speed and practicality.

What is bidirectional charging?

Bidirectional charging allows certain vehicles and systems to send power back to the home or grid. It is an important emerging technology for future energy flexibility.

Gutters

Seamless gutters, drainage sizing, leaf protection, and tile roof integration.

Why are seamless gutters better than sectional gutters?

Seamless gutters reduce the number of joints, which helps reduce leak points and creates a cleaner finished appearance.

What size gutters do I need?

The correct size depends on roof area, pitch, drainage concentration, and roofing type. Larger roofs and tile roofs often need larger gutter systems.

Do roof tiles need to be removed to install gutters?

On many tile roof systems, yes. Proper installation often requires lifting or removing lower courses so the gutter system can be installed correctly.

Do gutter guards work?

They can help reduce debris buildup, but they are not maintenance-free. The right product depends on the roof type, tree exposure, and drainage pattern.
